Welcome to the class webpage for the course Introduction to Partial Differential Equations.  Mathematical modeling has been a central methodology for physics and engineering for a long time, and the physical laws are written in terms partial differential equations; examples come from fluid dynamics (Navier-Stokes equations), solid mechanics (biharmonic or plate equation), electrodynamics (Maxwell's equations), thermodynamics (diffusion equation), etc. Partial Differential Equations have helped advance areas like probability theory, differential geometry, and Lie Algebras, to name a few. Partial Differential Equation modeling has played an important role in newer disciplines like financial engineering, image restoration and animation, neuroscience, spatial epidemiology, etc.
