Welcome to the class webpage for the second part of the course Mathematical Modeling .  Mathematical modeling has been a central methodology for the sciences and engineering for a long time, but has only been applied to areas of the social sciences in any significant way in the last century. In particular, modeling the spread of infectious diseases only started in the 1920s, but now is an important component of the area of epidemiology. In this short course segment we will concentrate on a deterministic approach (i.e. rate models involving differential equations) to the development of infectious disease mathematical models. This is partly to conform to other parts of the course, partly to minimize prerequisites needed for the course, and partly because it is easier to approach certain questions.

For more information on the course, please consult the course syllabus. Homework assignments, worked exercises, etc. will be posted here, so consult the webpage often throughout this segment of the course.
